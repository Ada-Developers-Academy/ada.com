What is Ada?

Ada Developers Academy is a not-for-profit software development school in Seattle, Washington that is exclusively for women. The program is for women who are passionate about starting a career in software development, but do not have previous computer science education or professional coding experience. The program is tuition-free and supported by the tech community as well as public funding.

What's the best way to contact Ada?

Please direct all questions and comments to contact@adadevelopersacademy.org.

How are Students Selected?

In the application, you'll be asked to create a video response to some pre-selected questions, conduct a technical reading assessment, solve a logic puzzle, and submit your resume. Based on your answers, you'll be invited to interview. Please join our mailing list to stay up to date with application deadlines.

What Will Class Be Like?

The academy features two sections: 24 weeks of intensive in-class instruction, followed by up to six months of internship at a Puget Sound region tech company. Class is every weekday, with holiday and vacation breaks.

Here's a typical day in class:

9:00 - 10:00
Instruction of new material
10:00 - 10:30
Daily assignments presented and explained
10:30 - 10:45
Break
10:45 - 12:30
Continued instruction
12:30 - 1:30
Lunch
1:30 - 2:30
Guest speaker
2:30 - 5:00
Project work
4:00 - 5:00
Student review with instructors

We assign daily homework and projects; anticipate 10-15 hours of study outside of the class per week.

Where will class be held?

Classes are in downtown Seattle, nearby lots of public transportation.

What Will I Learn?

You’ll come out of the academy with a full tool belt as a web developer. You’ll learn Ruby, Rails, HTML/CSS, and JavaScript. You’ll also leave with the know-how to pick up other programming languages.

How Much Does Ada Cost?

Ada Developers Academy has no tuition. Some students are eligible for scholarships for the duration of the class and internship.

Do I Need Any Supplies?

You will need a newer-model (less than one year old) Mac laptop. The majority of the support, documentation, and instructions are written with the Mac environment in mind. It's much easier to begin programming Ruby on a Mac, and we want all Ada students to be able to use the same materials without any hiccups.

We recommend a MacBook Air or a MacBook Pro. The base models of each (11" MacBook Air and the 13" MacBook Pro) are sufficient for class. If you are unable to purchase a laptop and require financial assistance, please note it on your application.

All books, software, and other materials will be provided by Ada.

Should I Apply?

If you have an interest in technology and want to transition into a career in software development, please apply. We’re looking for women without prior programming experience, but with high interest and aptitude.

What Kind of Job Can I Expect?

We’ll train you to become a full-stack developer, but very likely you’ll find a particular part of software development that suits you. You’ll be ready for a job as a web developer, whether it’s full-stack, front-end, QA, UX - it’s up to you.

Can I keep my job while attending Ada?

Ada is a full-time commitment (all-day class plus homework). If your employer is interested in sponsoring you and placing you in a development role, you can indicate this choice on the application.
