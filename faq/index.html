What is Ada?

Ada Developers Academy is a not-for-profit software development school in Seattle, Washington that is exclusively for women.

How are Students Selected?

Application submission will open in mid-September. You'll be asked to create a video response to some pre-selected questions, as well as submit your resume. Based on your answers, you'll be invited to interview.

When is Class?

Class start date will be announced later in September, 2013. The academy will feature two sections: 24 weeks of intensive in-class instruction, followed by up to six months of apprenticeship at a Puget Sound region tech company. Here's a typical day in class:

What Will I Learn?

You’ll come out of the academy with a full tool belt as a web developer. You’ll learn Ruby, Rails, HTML/CSS, and JavaScript. You’ll also leave with the know-how to pick up other programming languages.

How Much Does Ada Cost?

Ada Developers Academy has no tuition and students are eligible to receive a $1,000 monthly living stipend for the duration of the class and internship.

The best way to learn to program is to become immersed in it. Because Ada is not-for-profit, we have the ability to put students first. We want to attract the best possible candidates, regardless of financial situation, and make it as easy as possible to succeed.

Do I Need Any Supplies?

You will need a newer-model (less than one year old) Mac laptop. The majority of the support, documentation, and instructions are written with the Mac environment in mind. It's much easier to begin programming Ruby on a Mac, and we want all Ada students to be able to use the same materials without any hiccups.

We recommend a MacBook Air or a MacBook Pro. The base models of each (11" MacBook Air and the 13" MacBook Pro) are sufficient for class. If you are unable to purchase a laptop and require financial assistance, please note it on your application.

All books, software, and other materials will be provided by Ada.

Should I Apply?

If you have an interest in technology and want to transition into a career in software development, please apply! We’re looking for women without prior programming experience, but with high motivation and aptitude.

What Kind of Job Can I Expect?

We’ll train you to become a full-stack developer, but very likely you’ll find a particular part of software development that really suits you. You’ll be ready for a job as a web developer, whether it’s full-stack, front-end, QA, UX - it’s up to you!
